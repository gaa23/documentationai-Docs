## Assigning a package

Assigning a package to a patient creates a treatment plan for them based on a pre-built template. This is the fastest way to get a patient started on a protocol — the medication, dosage, titration schedule, pricing, and consent requirements are already defined in the package.

The patient receives a notification and must accept the plan before treatment begins.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the patient's profile">
    In the sidebar, select **Patients** and find the patient you want to treat. Select their name to open their profile.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assign a package">
    On the patient's profile, select **Assign Package**. A list of your clinic's available packages appears, grouped by category (e.g., Weight Loss, Anti-Aging, Performance).

    Select the package that matches the patient's treatment protocol.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and confirm">
    Review the package details — medication, dosage, titration schedule, and any required consent forms. Confirm to create the treatment plan.

    The patient is notified and will see the plan in their portal under **Treatments**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Patient accepts the plan">
    The treatment plan starts in **Pending Patient** status. The patient must review and accept the plan in their portal before it becomes active.

    If the package requires an intake questionnaire, the patient completes it after accepting. Your team then reviews and approves the intake before the plan moves to **Active**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Treatment plan statuses

| Status | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| Pending Patient | Waiting for the patient to accept the plan |
| Pending Patient Questionnaire | Patient needs to complete their intake form |
| Pending Doctor | Awaiting prescriber review and approval of the intake |
| Active | Treatment is underway |
| Paused | Treatment temporarily on hold |
| Completed | All treatments have been administered |

## Adding prescriber credentials

If this is the first treatment plan you're creating, you may be prompted to add your prescriber information — NPI number, DEA number, and state medical license. This is required to finalize any treatment plan. You can manage your credentials in **Settings → User Management**.

## Creating a custom treatment plan

If no existing package fits the patient's needs, you can build a plan from scratch. Go to **Treatment Plans → New** and define the medication, dosage, titration phases, and protocol manually, then attach it to the patient.
