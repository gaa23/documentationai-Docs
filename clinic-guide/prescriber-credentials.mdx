title: Set Up Prescriber Credentials
description: How to add your NPI, DEA number, and state medical licenses before creating treatment plans.
---

## Why credentials are required

Before you can create or finalize a treatment plan, the platform requires your prescriber credentials on file. This ensures every plan is associated with a licensed, verified prescriber.

You'll need to add these once. They are saved to your profile and auto-filled on future treatment plans.

## Adding your credentials

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to User Management">
    In the sidebar, select **Settings**, then **User Management**. Find your profile in the team member list and select **Edit**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter your NPI number">
    Your **NPI (National Provider Identifier)** is a 10-digit number issued by CMS. Enter it in the NPI field.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter your DEA number">
    Your **DEA number** is issued by the Drug Enforcement Administration and is required for prescribing controlled substances. Enter it in the DEA field.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add your state medical license">
    Add a license for each state where you see patients. For each license, enter:

    - **State** — the state the license is issued in
    - **License number** — your medical license number for that state
    - **Expiration date** — when the license expires

    Select **Add License** to add another state if you practice across multiple states.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save your profile">
    Select **Save**. Your credentials are now on file and will be used when you create treatment plans.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Multi-state practice

If you prescribe to patients in more than one state, add a license for each state. When creating a treatment plan, the platform uses the license that matches the patient's location. If no matching license is found, you'll be prompted to add one before the plan can be finalized.

## Keeping credentials current

License expiration dates are tracked in your profile. Update your credentials before they expire to avoid disruption to prescribing. Admins can view credential status for all prescribers in **Settings → User Management**.
