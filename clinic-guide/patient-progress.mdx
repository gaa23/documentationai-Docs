## What patients track

Patients on active treatment plans submit ongoing progress data through their portal:

- **Dose logs** — each time a patient administers a dose, they can log it with the actual amount given, how they felt, and any side effects
- **Vitals** — patients record measurements like weight and blood pressure over time
- **Target goals** — patients can set a target weight, giving you a benchmark to track against

Your team can review all of this from the clinic side on the patient's profile.

## Viewing a patient's progress

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the patient's profile">
    In the sidebar, select **Patients** and select the patient you want to review.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review vitals">
    The patient's vital signs history shows time-series data for weight, blood pressure, and any other tracked measurements. Use this to see trends over the course of their treatment.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review dose logs">
    The dose log shows each submitted administration record, including:

    - **Date and time** of administration
    - **Planned dose** vs. **actual dose administered**
    - **Patient-reported feeling** — rated on a scale from very poor to excellent
    - **Side effects reported** — any symptoms the patient flagged
    - **Notes** — free-text notes the patient added

    Look for patterns: consistent under-dosing, recurring side effects, or emotional responses that might indicate the protocol needs adjustment.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Reading the dose log

The feeling scale gives you a quick read on how a patient is tolerating treatment. A sustained run of poor ratings alongside specific side effects is a signal to follow up — either via [messaging](/clinic-guide/messaging) or by scheduling a consultation.

Side effects are patient-selected from a predefined list. Common ones to watch: nausea, fatigue, injection site reactions, and headache. If side effects are persistent or severe, consider [pausing or adjusting the treatment plan](/clinic-guide/adjust-treatment-plan).

## Following up

From the patient's profile you can:

- **Message the patient** directly to ask about their experience
- **Adjust the treatment plan** if the protocol needs to change
- **Book a follow-up consultation** from the Calendar
