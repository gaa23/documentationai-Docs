## Recent Updates

<Update label="March 2026" tags={["feature","improvement","bugfix"]}>
  ### Week of March 15–18

  - **New features**

    - Added a treatment plan overview that shows active plans, dose calendars, progress charts, and patient logs in one place.

    - Enabled Stripe-powered recurring billing with support for ongoing subscriptions.

  - **Improvements**

    - Refined commitment-based subscription plans so clinics can offer 3, 6, or 12‑month programs with clear discounts and refund guarantees.

  - **Fixes**

    - Improved reliability of subscription renewals and billing status to prevent missed or duplicated charges.

  ### Week of March 9–14

  - **New features**

    - Introduced a clinic billing dashboard with saved payment methods and payment history to simplify account management.

    - Modernized the pharmacy experience with updated layouts for medication dispensing and order review.

  - **Improvements**

    - Polished the signup and login experience, including multi‑clinic support so staff can switch between organizations without logging out.

  - **Fixes**

    - Resolved issues where some clinics could not complete signup, invite team members, or request demos because of edge‑case flows.

  ### Week of March 1–8

  - **New features**

    - Launched service‑specific intake questionnaires that adapt to multiple services, with conditional questions and automatic disqualification criteria.

    - Added configurable category entry flows so clinics can choose whether patients start with the calendar, packages, or chat.

    - Released a redesigned orders experience, including centralized order management and improved messaging between patients, clinics, and pharmacy.

    - Introduced a general 6‑step patient intake flow covering demographics, health history, interests, questionnaires, photo uploads, and scheduling.

  - **Improvements**

    - Improved photo handling throughout intake for IDs, body photos, and prescription images, with more reliable uploads and clearer guidance.

    - Added BMI and vitals tracking so clinics can follow key health metrics over time.

    - Enabled exporting completed intake data to PDF for easier charting and record keeping.

    - Overhauled the clinic dashboard with a setup tracker, key metrics, calendar view, and notification center.

  - **Fixes**

    - Tightened questionnaire validation and draft saving to prevent lost answers and incomplete submissions.

    - Addressed image upload edge cases, environment misconfigurations, and general stability issues across the platform.
</Update>

<Update label="February 2026" tags={["feature","improvement","bugfix"]}>
  ### Week of February 22–28

  - **New features**

    - Added clinic verification and license management so organizations can keep regulatory details up to date.

  - **Improvements**

    - Extended notification coverage and delivery, including push notifications, to keep providers and patients informed about time‑sensitive events.

  - **Fixes**

    - Removed deprecated clinic SKUs from selection flows to avoid confusion.

    - Improved reliability of the NewLife Pharmacy integration and hardened error handling for external services.

  ### Week of February 15–21

  - **New features**

    - Launched treatment actions so providers can accept, decline, or pause treatment plans directly from patient records.

    - Enabled provider assignment tools to route patients and treatment plans to the right clinician.

  - **Improvements**

    - Enhanced patient order history with clearer status indicators and timelines, making it easier to track fulfillment and follow‑ups.

    - Introduced bulk patient import via CSV to help clinics onboard existing populations quickly.

  - **Fixes**

    - Addressed edge cases in treatment transitions where status or ownership could become inconsistent.

  ### Week of February 8–14

  - **New features**

    - Rolled out patient messaging threads that keep all conversations in context for each patient and clinic.

    - Added inline booking from message threads so staff can schedule follow‑ups without leaving the conversation.

    - Introduced license tracking and compliance checks for bookings to ensure patients see properly licensed providers.

  - **Improvements**

    - Refined the patient overview page to surface health history, active treatments, orders, records, messaging, booking, and reassignment in a single view.

    - Enhanced order history and status views to support faster triage and support.

  - **Fixes**

    - Resolved message delivery and threading issues that could cause lost or duplicated conversations in busy clinics.

  ### Week of February 1–7

  - **New features**

    - Delivered a user and team management dashboard with role‑based access controls and email invites for staff.

    - Improved the scheduling experience with real‑time availability and a more intuitive time selection flow.

    - Added an accept‑invite flow so new team members can join clinics from secure email links.

    - Introduced treatment phases and dosing schemas with structured dose logs to support more complex protocols.

    - Redesigned the service and package creation experience, making it easier to define offerings, pricing, and fulfillment rules.

    - Launched the first ED‑specific intake form and the foundation for fully custom questionnaires.

  - **Improvements**

    - Tightened license checks during booking to prevent scheduling with out‑of‑jurisdiction providers.

  - **Fixes**

    - Fixed issues with NewLife Pharmacy reliability and improved platform‑wide stability and error messages when actions fail.
</Update>
